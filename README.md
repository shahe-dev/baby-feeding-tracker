# Baby Feeding Tracker - Progressive Web App

## Overview

A comprehensive Progressive Web App (PWA) designed for iPhone that helps track your baby's feeding based on evidence from:
- **Israeli LEAP Study** (81% peanut allergy reduction)
- **WHO 2023 Guidelines** (daily animal foods, iron requirements)
- **Scandinavian Research** (fatty fish, variety, whole foods)
- **New Zealand BLISS Study** (modified baby-led weaning safety)

## First-Time Setup

### 1. Create Baby Profile

1. Tap the **Profile** tab (far right)
2. Enter your baby's **name**
3. Enter **birth date** (this calculates age automatically)
4. Return to **Dashboard**

### 2. Add Initial Measurements

1. Go to **Progress** tab
2. Scroll to **"Add Measurements"**
3. Enter current **weight (kg)**
4. Enter current **height (cm)**
5. These will appear on your dashboard

### 3. Start Feeding Schedule

1. Go to **Meals** tab
2. Review **Week 1-2** plan (default)
3. Follow the daily meal suggestions
4. Tap any food to see benefits and research sources

## Daily Usage

### Logging a Feed

1. **Tap the "Log Feed" button** on dashboard
   - OR tap the **Log** tab (center bottom)

2. **Select meal type:**
   - Breakfast
   - Lunch
   - Dinner
   - Snack

3. **Select foods consumed:**
   - Tap each food your baby ate
   - Multiple selections allowed
   - Serving sizes shown automatically (age-adjusted)

4. **Enter amount:**
   - Example: "2 tbsp", "most of serving", "¬Ω bowl"

5. **Add notes (optional):**
   - Reactions, preferences, observations

6. **Tap "Save Feed Entry"**

### Viewing Meal Plans

1. Go to **Meals** tab
2. Select week/month at top:
   - Weeks 1-2 (5-6 months)
   - Weeks 3-4 (6.5-7 months)
   - Month 2 (7 months)

3. View **daily schedule** with meals
4. **Tap any food** to expand details:
   - Benefits
   - Research sources
   - Allergen info
   - Serving size

5. Orange boxes show **allergen introductions**

### Monitoring Allergens

### Tracking Progress

1. Go to **Progress** tab

5. Add new measurements as needed

## Key Evidence-Based Principles

### 1. Early Allergen Introduction (Israeli LEAP)
- Introduce peanut, egg, sesame, fish, tree nuts between 4-6 months
- **Sustained exposure required:** 3x weekly minimum
- **81% reduction in peanut allergy** with this approach

### 2. Daily Animal Foods (WHO 2023)
- Meat, fish, or eggs **every day**
- Heme iron from meat: 25% absorption
- Fortified cereals: only 10% absorption
- **2.5x better iron from meat**

### 3. Healthy Fats (All Studies)
- No restriction under age 2
- DHA from fatty fish for brain development
- Olive oil, tahini, avocado, egg yolks
- Brain grows 260% in first 2 years

### 4. Dairy-Free Adaptation
- All meal plans exclude dairy (cow milk protein allergy)
- Calcium from: tahini, leafy greens, sardines with bones
- Healthy fats from: olive oil, avocado, nut butters, fish

## Troubleshooting

### App Won't Install
- Must use **Safari** (not Chrome or other browsers)
- iOS must be up-to-date
- Try closing Safari and reopening

### Offline Not Working
- Load the app once with internet
- Service worker needs to cache resources first
- After first load, works completely offline

### Data Not Saving
- Check iPhone storage space
- Safari settings: allow local storage
- Clear Safari cache and reload app

### Charts Not Showing
- Add at least 2 data points for trend lines
- Weight and height need multiple measurements
- Allergen chart needs at least one week of feeds

### Food Details Won't Expand
- Try tapping directly on the food item
- Scroll if needed to see expanded content
- Check for iOS updates

## Recommended Workflow

### Week 1-2 (Starting at 5-6 months)

**Day 1:**
1. Open **Meals** tab, review Day 1 plan
2. Prepare: Pureed beef + sweet potato + olive oil
3. Introduce peanut (1/4 tsp thinned)
4. After feeding, go to **Log** tab
5. Select foods consumed, enter amount
6. Save entry
7. Check **Dashboard** - allergen tracker updates

**Day 4:**
1. Review Day 4 plan in **Meals**
2. Prepare meal following suggestions
3. Introduce egg (allergen intro shown in orange box)
4. Log the feed
5. Continue daily pattern

**End of Week 1:**
1. Check **Dashboard** allergen tracker
2. Verify peanut appears 3x in the week
3. Adjust following week if needed

### Ongoing (Month 2+)

**Daily:**
- Review meal plan for the day
- Prepare and serve meals
- Log each feed
- Monitor allergen exposure

**Weekly:**
- Check **Progress** tab for allergen chart
- Ensure all allergens ‚â•3x
- Review weight/height trends
- Add new measurements if due

**Monthly:**
- Update to next meal plan phase
- Add weight and height measurements
- Review growth charts
- Adjust serving sizes (automatic by age)
